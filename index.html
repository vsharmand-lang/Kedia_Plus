The Algorithm
Vijay Kedia's SMILE Framework → DeDollarisation Filter → Buying Signal Engine
This monitor implements a 4-stage stock selection and alert system. Stage 1 applies Kedia's SMILE framework to the NSE universe. Stage 2 removes survival-risk companies. Stage 3 filters for 14%+ 10-year return potential. Stage 4 applies the DeDollarisation safety test — the most rigorous filter, ensuring the portfolio is structurally immune to dollar collapse. The result is 4 high-conviction, long-term compounders monitored 24/7 for buying opportunities.
Stage 1 — SMILE Framework (Vijay Kedia)
S
Small in Size
Market cap below ₹15,000 cr at time of selection. Small enough to have a long growth runway ahead. Large caps have already been discovered — the multi-bagger opportunity is in the small cap universe where institutional coverage is thin and price discovery is inefficient.
Market Cap < ₹15,000 CrBelow Nifty 250
M
Medium in Experience
Company has 8–25 years of operating history. Old enough to have a proven business model and survived at least one full economic cycle. Young enough that the management still has ambition to grow. Avoids both untested startups and mature businesses that have hit their growth ceiling.
8–25 Years Operating HistorySurvived 1+ Economic Cycle
I
Large in Ambition (Inspiration)
Management is thinking at 5–10x the current scale. Evidence: aggressive capex plans, international expansion, R&D investment, promoter increasing stake. Kedia studies promoters as closely as financial statements — management quality and honesty is the ultimate moat.
Promoter Increasing StakeExpansion Plans VisibleClean Governance Record
L
Large Market Potential
The addressable market must be large enough to allow 10–15x revenue growth from current levels. Niche monopolies in expanding global categories qualify (e.g. EV shunts, railway safety systems). Declining or saturated industries are automatically disqualified regardless of other metrics.
TAM > 10x Current RevenueStructural Growth Sector
E
Extra-large Earnings Growth Potential
Consistent revenue and profit growth. ROE above 18%, low debt (D/E below 0.5), operating leverage visible in margins. The business should be able to grow earnings at 20–30% CAGR for the next 5–10 years driven by the structural tailwind in its sector.
ROE > 18%D/E < 0.5Revenue CAGR > 15%PAT CAGR > 20%
Stage 2 — Survival Filter
2
Remove Existential Threats
Eliminate any company facing technology disruption (ICE auto components in an EV world), structural market decline (print publishing, traditional textiles), or management integrity failures. A great SMILE score is worthless if the business model becomes obsolete within 5 years.
No EV disruption riskNo structural declineNo governance red flags
Stage 3 — Return Potential Filter
3
14%+ CAGR Required Over 10 Years
Entry price relative to intrinsic value matters. Calculated as: Estimated EPS in Year 10 × Estimated Exit PE × Safety Margin. If the current market price implies less than 14% annualised return at base case earnings growth, the stock is disqualified regardless of business quality — even great businesses can be bad investments at wrong prices.
Base Case CAGR > 14%Valuation-adjusted entry
Stage 4 — DeDollarisation Safety Test (3 Tests)
T1
No Critical Dollar Trade Settlement Dependency
Revenues must either be in INR, or the company must have monopoly pricing power to renegotiate currency terms. Simple USD/EUR exporters without pricing power fail this test — in a dollar collapse, trade finance freezes and payment collection becomes impossible.
INR Revenue PrimaryOR Monopoly Repricing Power
T2
Demand Not Tied to US/Western Economic Health
The underlying demand driver must be independent of US/EU consumer spending or corporate capital allocation. Indian government mandates, domestic infrastructure, global EV adoption, and commodity demand from emerging markets all qualify. US luxury consumer spending does not.
India domestic demandOR EM/EV structural demand
T3
Business Structurally Strengthens in Multipolar World
The business must not merely survive dedollarisation — it must benefit from it. India's manufacturing push accelerates, defence spending rises, EV adoption speeds up, and commodity prices surge when dollar hegemony fades. Stocks that are direct beneficiaries of these trends pass; those merely insulated from harm do not.
Direct multipolar beneficiary
Stage 5 — Buying Signal Engine (This Monitor)
5
Price Drop = Opportunity, Not Risk
For businesses that have passed all 4 stages above, a temporary price drop is a buying opportunity — not a warning signal. The monitor tracks drops from: (a) 52-week high, (b) 30-day moving average, (c) 90-day moving average. Alerts fire at configurable thresholds. The Kedia philosophy: price corrections in high-conviction businesses are gifts, not threats. The algorithm exists to ensure you never miss one.
